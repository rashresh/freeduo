5 CONTENT CREATION HACKS YOU MUST KNOW

By Reshmi Menon15 Aug 2020

Image Source: Freepik

So many platforms and so many ideas flooding in your mind. Do you feel confused? Well, I can totally relate to it. Sometimes you have that block as to what to write and which platform to focus on etc. So here I’m bringing few easy content creation hacks. Let’s dive in.

Choose that one platform where you will post consistently no matter what. It can be video platforms like Youtube if you loving being in front of the camera or content creation platforms like Instagram and Facebook where you can socialize directly with your audience or blogs if you prefer writing long articles.

Rather than writing about topics that you care about, understand the topics that your audience would love to see. I guess all content creators face this issue. Finding the right balance between what you love to talk about and what the audience actually wants to hear. So to understand your audience, talk to them, send out google forms, conduct Ask me now’s, search in Google, do keyword analysis. The key is to understand what your customer wants to listen and when your content starts revolving around your audience then they will engage with it, share it and that content will get way too many likes, comments, subscriptions etc.

Using the same content in different forms. In marketing language, this is known as repurposing. So you wrote that amazing blog, now take out a quote, edit it into a great visual in Canva and post on your Instagram and Facebook, similarly, you can make an interesting video out of it and post it on Youtube and IGTV. So this way you will be able to reach out to different platforms with the same content.

Have a solid content calendar in place. Rather than posting on the go have a clear picture in mind as to how you are going to post the next week or the next month. I will recommend planning for any time between 30 to 60 days because beyond that you might have some other plans and thus it is not advisable to plan way too ahead for your content as you always need to be posting the latest trends. So chart out what all kinds of posts are you planning to put up, like on Instagram I plan out the posts I will be putting up, the days I will be giving shoutouts, when am I planning on an Ask Me Anything session etc

Start batching. Rather than creating the content just before the posting day, sit at one stretch and create your content in batches, maybe you can write 3 blogs a day or create 2 video contents in a day, in this way you are making a cluster of similar work and focussing all your attention to it and finishing it off at one stretch.

So whichever platform you choose, consistency, and quality content are the two key requirements to attract an audience.
